EazyTheory: Learning the Staff & Note Placements

The Treble Cleff is comprised of seven notes with five lines and four spaces. Each note occupies a line or space of the staff.

Sharps and Flats indicate a higher or lower pitch

in semitone of the given note.

Note placements on the staff affect what melody is produced and how its sounds. Other factors also impact the sound such as rests, different notes (eighth, whole, sixteenth, etc.), staccatos, accents, and the key signature.
